## Immediate Build Queue
- Build a business plan that aligns operations, funding, storytelling, and hardware development.
- Build a media kit that can support sponsorship outreach, speaking requests, and press conversations.
- Build a sponsorship package that explains what miniBIOTA offers, what surfaces exist, and how a partner would be represented.
- Start a speaking and pitching workflow instead of treating events as a one-off task.
- Establish the newsletter handoff from `email_signups` to an external email platform.

## Minimum Pipeline Structure To Create
- Opportunity or organization name
- Opportunity type: sponsorship, speaking, event, donation, affiliate, or other
- Current status
- Next action
- Next action date
- Owner
- Estimated value or strategic importance
- Supporting assets needed

## Cross-Functional Inputs Required
- Company Operations: business plan, priority alignment, and decision context
- Brand & Marketing: sponsor-facing language, founder narrative, and positioning
- Content Production: proof assets, visuals, and story examples for outreach
- Web & Digital Presence: donation, events, affiliate, and newsletter capture surfaces
- Finance & Budget: revenue baseline, overhead picture, liability context, and estimated value assumptions for opportunities

## Completion Signals
- The business plan exists in durable notes rather than as an implied need.
- The media kit and sponsorship package exist as reusable assets.
- At least one working relationship tracker exists in the vault.
- The newsletter capture path has a defined downstream workflow.
- Partnership and sales work can be briefed to a Gem or human without reconstructing the function from scratch.

## Notes
- This backlog is about building the operating system for commercial activity, not claiming that the system already exists.
- Once these basics exist, the next step is prioritizing target partner categories and active outreach sequences.
- The CFO readiness work should stay linked to this backlog so partner and sponsorship design is grounded in real cost pressure and revenue goals.
