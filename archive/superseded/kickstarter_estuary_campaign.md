## The Anchor Concept

For the first time, miniBIOTA's freshwater and saltwater worlds will connect through a living salinity gradient. The estuary is not a product. It is an experiment documenting the transition zone where life must adapt or die, in real time, inside a sealed system no one has built like this before.

This campaign is not asking people to buy something. It is inviting them to help create a living world and leave a trace inside it.

Campaign question: "How small can a world be and still remain alive?"

Scientific hook: the estuary transition zone opens a new line of ecological knowledge with direct practical applications, including naturalistic amano shrimp breeding inside a fully documented, self-sustaining system.

## Campaign Structure

Funding goal: $15,000, described as covering hardware, enclosure fabrication, sensor integration, and the first 90 days of the estuary build documented on camera.

Campaign duration: 30 days, no extensions.

Launch sequence:
- 6 weeks before launch: warmup content begins; estuary teaser video; Patreon members see design sketches first.
- 2 weeks before launch: Kickstarter preview page live; email list notified; Discord members get early access to the pledge page.
- Launch day: video drops simultaneously on YouTube and Kickstarter.
- Week 2: mid-campaign update video showing early build progress.
- Final 48 hours: urgency push across all channels.

## Reward Tiers

### Tier 1 - Observer ($15)
- Early access to all estuary videos before YouTube release.
- Backer-only Field Journal posts.
- Name listed permanently on the Founding Backers page on the website.
- Zero physical fulfillment.

### Tier 2 - Participant ($50)
- Everything in Tier 1.
- Vote on key decisions from a scientifically vetted shortlist.
- Monthly backer-only livestream walkthrough.
- Access to Director's Cut videos.

### Tier 3 - Legacy ($150)
- Everything in Tier 2.
- Name permanently embedded inside the estuary build.
- Digital certificate showing where their name exists inside miniBIOTA.
- Name spoken aloud in the build video when that section is installed.
- No shipping or inventory.

### Tier 4 - Steward ($300)
- Everything in Tier 3.
- Biome Steward or Species Steward status.
- Name appears on-screen whenever their biome or species group is featured.
- Quarterly deep-dive update focused on their biome or species.

### Tier 5 - Architect ($1,000, limited to 10)
- Everything in Tier 4.
- Name a non-species structure inside the estuary.
- Credited as Founding Architect in every major video from the estuary build forward.
- One private recorded Q&A about design philosophy and tradeoffs.

### Tier 6 - Worldbuilder ($5,000, limited to 3)
- Everything in Tier 5.
- Executive Producer credit on the estuary build series.
- Name or logo permanently etched into the main system frame.
- Dedicated episode documenting the component or expansion their support enabled.
- Annual private system walkthrough video.

### Add-On - The Estuary Field Notes ($75)
- Comprehensive digital field guide documenting the estuary build process.
- Covers design decisions, failures, salinity calibration methodology, species selection rationale, transition zone ecology, and long-term outcomes.
- Written in field-note style.
- Delivered 6 months post-campaign when outcomes are documented.
- No printing or shipping.

## Campaign Video Requirements

Draft structure:

1. Open with existing footage: 30 seconds, no narration, just sound.
2. Ask: "How small can a world be and still remain alive?"
3. Explain what miniBIOTA is, the existing system, and what has been proven.
4. Explain the estuary build, the science, and why it is different.
5. State what support enables.
6. Explain tiers in 90 seconds.
7. Close with the question again.

Key constraint: no overproduction; keep the field-note aesthetic.

## Pre-Campaign Warmup Content

- Week 1: teaser post about designing something for a long time.
- Week 2: design sketch reveal on Patreon and Discord.
- Week 3: short video explaining salinity transition zones.
- Week 4: first render or mock-up.
- Week 5: email list gets early access to the preview page.
- Week 6: launch.
