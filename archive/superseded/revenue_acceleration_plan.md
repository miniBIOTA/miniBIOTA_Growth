## Tier 1 - Immediate

### Patreon Push
Current: $88/mo. Target: $300-500/mo.
25,000 subscribers. Industry conversion is 0.5-1%. You are at 0.07%.

Draft actions:
- Record one dedicated video about the Patreon and what it funds.
- Pin a comment on recent videos.
- Add a "What your support enables" section to the channel description.
- Mention Patreon in video outros with a specific reason.

### Ko-fi Placement
- Add to video descriptions as a one-time support option beneath Patreon.

## Tier 2 - Month 1-2

### First Sponsorship Deal
Source assumptions:
- 25k subscribers in a niche audience.
- Possible $500-1,500 per video sponsorship range.
- Media kit needed.
- Target category ideas: aquarium hardware, grow light companies, scientific equipment, naturalist supply brands, science education platforms.
- Cold pitch cadence idea.

### Course Pre-Sale
Source assumptions:
- Course concept: "Building miniBIOTA - A Field Guide to Closed-System Ecology".
- Early-bird prices: $97, $147, $197.
- Delivery timeline: 90 days from pre-sale close.
- Revenue estimate: $2,000-10,000.

Draft module outline:
1. Why closed systems fail.
2. Designing for self-regulation.
3. Water chemistry without intervention.
4. Introducing organisms.
5. Live telemetry on a budget.
6. The lake biome case study.
7. The estuary transition zone.

## Tier 3 - Month 2-4

### Kickstarter - The Estuary Build
Source assumptions:
- Funding goal: $15,000.
- Realistic range: $22,000-75,000+.
- Timeline: 6 weeks warmup + 30-day campaign.
- Zero physical fulfillment.
- All reward delivery digital or in-video.

## Tier 4 - Post-Kickstarter

### Merch - Print-on-Demand
Source assumptions:
- Platform: Printful or Printify.
- Product direction: scientific, not fan merch.
- Ideas: illustration prints, data visualization poster, organism field guide card sets, Founding Observer hoodie or tee.
- Revenue estimate: $200-600/mo.

### Course Delivery
Source framed delivery as editing and structuring existing content.

## What Not To Build Right Now
- Speaking circuit after Kickstarter closes.
- Press outreach tied to Kickstarter warmup.
- Reddit strategy during Kickstarter warmup.
- Substack newsletter sync alongside Patreon push.
- Native site comments after consistent traffic justifies it.
