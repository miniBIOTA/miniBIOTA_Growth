## Scope
- Defines how miniBIOTA turns public interest, technical credibility, and storytelling into durable business support.
- Covers sponsorships, partner outreach, speaking and event opportunities, audience-support conversion paths, and the systems used to track them.

## Current State
- This function is still early, but it is no longer blank: the vault already contains commercial signals across operations, brand, and web documentation.
- Known near-term deliverables include a business plan, media kit, sponsorship package, and speaking/pitching activity, but they are not yet organized into one operating system.
- The website already has commercial and audience-capture surfaces through `/donate`, `/events`, `/build`, and newsletter signup routes, while `email_signups` remains capture-only until an external platform sync is defined.

## Key Facts
- Commercial readiness depends on aligning business planning, brand positioning, content proof, and public conversion surfaces.
- The brand is already framed to attract audiences, partners, donors, and media attention.
- The `/build` route is intended for affiliate-backed hardware and resource content.
- No partner pipeline, pricing structure, sponsorship tiers, or CRM-style operating cadence is yet documented.

## Priorities
- Turn scattered commercial signals into a working operating model covering sponsorships, speaking, audience support, and affiliate-backed resources.
- Build core commercial assets: business plan, media kit, sponsorship package, and a lightweight relationship pipeline.
- Connect newsletter capture, website conversion routes, brand messaging, and content proof into one repeatable outreach workflow.

## Risks / Gaps
- Without an explicit commercial system, public interest can accumulate without conversion.
- Newsletter-sync and relationship-management workflow missing.
- Ownership and sequencing can blur because this function spans multiple domains.
