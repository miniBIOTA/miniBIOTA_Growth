## Anchor Concept
The source campaign concept framed the estuary build as the first connection between miniBIOTA's freshwater and saltwater worlds through a living salinity gradient.

Draft positioning preserved from the source:

- The estuary is not a product.
- It is an experiment documenting a transition zone where life adapts or dies in real time.
- The campaign asks supporters to help create a living world and leave a trace inside it.

Draft campaign question:

> How small can a world be and still remain alive?

Draft scientific hook:

- The estuary transition zone could open a line of ecological knowledge with practical applications, including naturalistic amano shrimp breeding inside a documented, self-sustaining system.
- The source claimed this had not been done at this level of documentation.

Treat this hook as draft/reference language, not an approved public claim.

## Draft Campaign Structure

### Funding Goal Idea
Source funding goal idea: $15,000.

The source said this would cover hardware, enclosure fabrication, sensor integration, and the first 90 days of the estuary build documented on camera.

The source also described the goal as conservative, potentially achievable within 72 hours if warmup was done correctly, with additional funds going to stretch goals. Treat all of that as a draft scenario, not a financial projection or public promise.

### Campaign Duration Idea
Source campaign duration idea: 30 days, no extensions.

This is a draft timing assumption, not an approved launch plan.

### Launch Sequence Idea
Draft sequence preserved from the source:

- 6 weeks before launch: warmup content begins, estuary teaser video, and Patreon members see design sketches first.
- 2 weeks before launch: Kickstarter preview page live, email list notified, and Discord members get early access to the pledge page.
- Launch day: video drops simultaneously on YouTube and Kickstarter.
- Week 2: mid-campaign update video showing early build progress.
- Final 48 hours: urgency push across all channels.

This is not an approved launch sequence, schedule, platform action, or public communication plan.

## Draft Reward Tiers

All reward tiers, names, prices, benefits, limits, and fulfillment descriptions are draft/reference only.

### Tier 1: Observer
Source price idea: $15.

Draft positioning: for people who want to watch history unfold.

Draft benefits:

- Early access to estuary videos before YouTube release.
- Backer-only Field Journal posts with observations, failures, and course corrections.
- Name listed permanently on the Founding Backers page on the website.
- Zero physical fulfillment.

### Tier 2: Participant
Source price idea: $50.

Draft positioning: for people who want agency in what happens next.

Draft benefits:

- Everything in Tier 1.
- Vote on key decisions from scientifically vetted shortlists.
- Monthly backer-only livestream walkthrough of the full ecosystem.
- Access to Director's Cut videos deeper than public YouTube content.

### Tier 3: Legacy
Source price idea: $150.

Draft positioning: the source described this as the campaign magic.

Draft benefits:

- Everything in Tier 2.
- Name permanently embedded inside the estuary build, such as engraved on a biome access panel, embedded in a 3D-printed internal structural element, or inscribed on a visible Biosphere Wall inside the system.
- Digital certificate showing where the name exists inside miniBIOTA.
- Name spoken aloud in the build video when that section is installed.
- No shipping or inventory.

### Tier 4: Steward
Source price idea: $300.

Draft positioning: adoption without ownership, mirroring conservation sponsorship.

Draft benefits:

- Everything in Tier 3.
- Biome Steward or Species Steward status associated with a biome or organism group.
- Name appears on-screen whenever that biome or species group is featured.
- Quarterly deep-dive update focused on that biome or species.

### Tier 5: Architect
Source price idea: $1,000, limited to 10.

Draft positioning: part of the design lineage.

Draft benefits:

- Everything in Tier 4.
- Name a non-species structure inside the estuary, such as a chamber, loop, channel, or system node.
- Credited as Founding Architect in major videos from the estuary build forward.
- One private recorded Q&A walking through design philosophy and tradeoffs.

### Tier 6: Worldbuilder
Source price idea: $5,000, limited to 3.

Draft positioning: patronage, not commerce.

Draft benefits:

- Everything in Tier 5.
- Executive Producer credit on the estuary build series.
- Name or logo permanently etched into the main system frame.
- Dedicated episode documenting the component or expansion their support enabled.
- Annual private system walkthrough video recorded and sent to them.

Source target audience idea: museums, science educators, science-aligned companies, and conservation-minded donors.

### Add-On: The Estuary Field Notes
Source price idea: $75, available to any backer at any tier.

Draft concept:

- Comprehensive digital field guide documenting the estuary build process.
- Covers design decisions, failures, salinity calibration methodology, species selection rationale, transition zone ecology, and long-term outcomes.
- Written in field-note style.
- Delivered 6 months post-campaign when outcomes are documented.
- No printing or shipping.

This is a draft add-on idea and not an approved product, price, delivery promise, or fulfillment obligation.

## Draft Campaign Video Requirements

The source stated that backers cannot touch the ecosystem, so the campaign video should make them feel like they can see into another world.

Draft structure:

1. Open with the best existing footage from the system: 30 seconds, no narration, just sound.
2. Ask the campaign question.
3. Explain what miniBIOTA is, the existing system, and what has been proven.
4. Explain the estuary build, the science, and why the source believed it had not been done this way.
5. State what support enables.
6. Explain tiers in 90 seconds, visually and cleanly.
7. Close with the campaign question again.

Draft constraint: no overproduction; preserve the authentic field-note aesthetic.

This is creative direction, not approved campaign copy or claim-reviewed public content.

## Draft Pre-Campaign Warmup

The source proposed a 6-week warmup across channels and said not to announce Kickstarter immediately.

Draft sequence:

- Week 1: teaser post about designing something for a long time, with no details.
- Week 2: design sketch reveal on Patreon and Discord, members first.
- Week 3: short video explaining salinity transition zones and why they matter.
- Week 4: first render or mock-up.
- Week 5: email list gets early access to the preview page.
- Week 6: launch.

This is draft sequencing only, not an approved launch schedule or public communication plan.
