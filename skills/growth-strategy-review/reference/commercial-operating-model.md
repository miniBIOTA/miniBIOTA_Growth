# Commercial Operating Model Reference

## Use
Use this reference with `skills/growth-strategy-review/SKILL.md` when reviewing commercial direction, strategic fit, current surfaces, planned lanes, dependencies, or unresolved operating-model questions.

This is internal model context. It does not create offers, prices, sponsor obligations, partner commitments, public claims, launch dates, financial commitments, legal commitments, sales commitments, roadmap commitments, or approved strategy.

## Purpose
- Define how miniBIOTA turns mission, technical credibility, and public interest into durable business support.
- Keep current-state commercial signals separate from future-state commercial design.

## Current Signals
- The company-level task list calls for a business plan, media kit, sponsorship package, and speaking/pitching activity.
- The brand layer frames miniBIOTA as a project that can attract audiences, partners, donors, and media attention.
- Website strategy and information architecture expose public conversion surfaces through donation, events, build/resource, and newsletter/signup routes.
- The web platform captures newsletter signups into `email_signups`; external provider sync remains an open operational step.

## Current Commercial Surfaces
- `/donate`: direct support surface.
- `/events`: support for speaking, appearances, and public activations.
- `/build`: affiliate-backed hardware and resource content surface.
- `/subscribe` and `/signup`: audience-capture paths for newsletter growth.

These are current surfaces, not approved offers, prices, sponsorship packages, partner obligations, or campaign commitments.

## Planned Near-Term Lanes
- Sponsorships and brand partnerships around the biosphere story, engineering visibility, and public-facing documentation.
- Speaking and event appearances that translate the founder story, ecological logic, and technical build into partner-facing opportunities.
- Audience-supported revenue paths such as donation, Patreon-adjacent membership flow, and newsletter-driven conversion.
- Affiliate-backed build resources where the hardware journey can support a monetization-adjacent content surface.

Treat these as planned internal lanes unless a higher-priority source explicitly approves execution, outreach, pricing, or public commitments.

## Core Dependencies
- Business plan: needed so commercial outreach is tied to operating reality rather than generic pitch language.
- Brand: sponsor-facing language, founder narrative, and positioning.
- Content: credible proof assets, visuals, story examples, and system progress.
- Web: donation, events, affiliate, campaign, and newsletter capture surfaces.
- Financials: revenue baseline, overhead picture, liability context, and estimated value assumptions.
- Pipeline: lightweight relationship tracking so commercial follow-through is not lost across chats and scattered notes.

## Current Unknowns
- No pricing model, sponsorship tiers, or partner package structure is approved here.
- No standard owner, cadence, or CRM-style workflow is defined here.
- No explicit rule yet defines how email capture, website conversion, and outbound partner follow-up connect operationally.
