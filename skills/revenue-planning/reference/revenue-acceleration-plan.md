# Revenue Acceleration Plan Reference

## Use
Use this reference with `skills/revenue-planning/SKILL.md` when evaluating revenue channels, sequencing, time-to-first-dollar, scenario assumptions, or historical revenue planning detail.

This is internal planning reference only. It does not create public offers, prices, sponsor terms, partner obligations, campaign promises, launch dates, financial commitments, sales commitments, active projects, tasks, approved strategy, or revenue commitments.

All numbers, timelines, targets, prices, conversion rates, subscriber/email counts, and projections below are historical planning assumptions from the source document unless freshly verified through Financials, Brain, Supabase, or explicit user direction.

## Historical Financial Gap Context
Source assumptions:

| Item | Historical Amount |
|---|---:|
| Average monthly content revenue | $460 |
| Monthly debt minimums | $1,695 |
| Monthly deficit before OpEx | -$1,235 |

The source conclusion was that content revenue alone would not close the gap and that new revenue streams should be evaluated by time-to-first-dollar. Verify all values before using them as current.

## Sequencing Logic
The source plan sequenced revenue by time-to-first-dollar:

1. Immediate support levers: Patreon and Ko-fi.
2. Month 1-2 levers: first sponsorship deal and course pre-sale validation.
3. Month 2-4 lever: Kickstarter for the estuary build.
4. Post-Kickstarter levers: merch and course delivery.

Use this as a planning heuristic, not an approved schedule.

## Channel Notes
### Patreon And Ko-fi
Source assumptions included Patreon at $88/mo, target range of $300-500/mo, 25,000 subscribers, and a claimed conversion gap. Suggested actions included a dedicated Patreon video, pinned comments, channel description support language, recurring video mentions, and Ko-fi placement in video descriptions.

Treat all values, targets, channel copy, and execution steps as assumptions or draft ideas unless explicitly approved.

### Sponsorship
Source assumptions included a possible $500-1,500 per-video sponsorship range, 25k subscribers, niche audience positioning, media kit need, 10-15 aligned brand categories, and a cold-pitch cadence idea.

Treat sponsorship values, brand categories, outreach cadence, pitch hooks, and close timing as unapproved planning assumptions. Do not imply sponsor interest, sponsor terms, sponsor obligations, or approved outreach.

### Course Pre-Sale
Source assumptions included a course concept, module outline, early-bird prices of $97, $147, and $197, a 90-day delivery timeline, a 308-person email list, and a possible $2,000-10,000 pre-sale range.

Treat prices, delivery timelines, course claims, conversion assumptions, and waitlist/public-page work as unapproved unless current user direction explicitly approves them.

### Kickstarter
Source assumptions included a $15,000 funding goal, $22,000-75,000 realistic range, 6-week warmup, 30-day campaign, zero physical fulfillment, and digital/in-video reward delivery.

Treat all Kickstarter details as draft campaign/revenue assumptions unless approved through campaign planning. Use `skills/campaign-planning/SKILL.md` and the active Kickstarter reference when campaign details are central.

### Merch
Source assumptions included print-on-demand via Printful or Printify, scientific illustration product direction, data visualization posters, organism field guide card sets, and a Founding Observer hoodie or tee. Revenue estimate was $200-600/mo ongoing after launch wave.

Treat product ideas, vendors, designs, and revenue estimates as draft assumptions.

### Course Delivery
The source framed course delivery as structuring existing video content rather than starting from zero. Treat this as a planning observation, not an approved product commitment.
