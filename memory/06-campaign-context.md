# Campaign Context

## Scope
Campaign work includes Kickstarter, launch sequencing, reward ideas, warmup content, campaign messaging, risk review, and post-campaign commercial sequencing.

## Current Planning Material
The detailed estuary Kickstarter source has been migrated to `skills/campaign-planning/reference/kickstarter-estuary-campaign.md`.

That reference describes an estuary build campaign concept, positioning, supporter assumptions, reward tier ideas, pricing ideas, launch sequence ideas, stretch-goal ideas, fulfillment notes, risk/dependency implications, campaign video structure, warmup content, revenue scenarios, and post-campaign sequencing. Treat all of it as draft/reference unless explicitly approved.

## Campaign Concept Context
- The migrated campaign concept centers on an estuary build connecting freshwater and saltwater worlds through a living salinity gradient.
- The positioning frames the campaign as support for a documented living-world experiment, not as a product sale.
- The migrated campaign question and scientific hook are draft messaging/reference only; they are not approved public claims.
- The estuary concept belongs to the build and content strategy, but campaign launch, funding, reward, fulfillment, and public claim decisions remain unapproved unless separately authorized.

- Stretch goal ideas and unlock language.
- Campaign video requirements and draft structure.

## Required Framing
Campaign material must distinguish:
- Current facts.
- Draft campaign concepts.
- Assumptions.
- Unverified numbers.
- Draft reward ideas.
- Draft prices and funding goals.
- Draft launch sequences and timelines.
- Draft fulfillment notes.
- Recommendations.
- Approved campaign strategy, only when explicitly verified.
- Approved public promises.
- Risks and dependencies.
- Decisions required from the user.

## Boundaries
- Do not present campaign tiers, prices, launch timing, stretch goals, deliverables, or public messaging as approved commitments unless explicitly approved.
- Do not create platform records, campaign pages, backer records, or public-facing copy without approval.
- Do not promise delivery timelines, reward fulfillment, sponsor treatment, or public claims without explicit approval.
- Do not imply a Kickstarter launch is approved, scheduled, live, or funded.
- Do not imply reward tiers, add-ons, prices, backer benefits, stretch goals, or fulfillment paths are promised.
- Do not treat campaign copy or scientific hook language as public-ready without Brand and claim review.
