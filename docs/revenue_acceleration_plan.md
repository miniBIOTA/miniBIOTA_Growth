## Tier 1 — Immediate (This Week)

Current: $88/mo. Target: $300–500/mo.

**What to do:**

- Add a "What your support enables" section to the channel description

This is already decided strategy — it just needs to be executed.

## Tier 2 — Month 1–2

### First Sponsorship Deal
With 25k subscribers in a tight niche (ecology, closed systems, DIY science), you can command $500–1,500 per video. This is not a high volume play — one deal per month changes the monthly math significantly.

**What to do:**
- Build a one-page media kit: subscriber count, engagement rate, niche description, audience profile
- Identify 10–15 brands that align: aquarium hardware, grow light companies, scientific equipment, naturalist supply brands, science education platforms
- Send 5 cold pitches per week starting month 1
- Target: first deal closed by week 6

The estuary build is the pitch hook — "I'm documenting a first-of-its-kind estuary build. This is the audience you want."

### Course Pre-Sale
**Do not build the course first. Pre-sell it.**

A waitlist + early-bird offer at $97–197 can generate $2,000–10,000 before a single lesson is recorded. The pre-sale validates the concept and funds the production.

**Course concept:** "Building miniBIOTA — A Field Guide to Closed-System Ecology"

Audience: people who watch the channel and want to build their own version. Niche but passionate. There is no comparable course for naturalistic closed biosphere systems at this depth.

**Module outline:**
1. Why closed systems fail — the 10 most common mistakes
2. Designing for self-regulation — substrate, cycling, microbiome foundations
3. Water chemistry without intervention — passive balance vs active management
4. Introducing organisms — sequencing, compatibility, load logic
5. Live telemetry on a budget — sensors, data logging, what to watch for
6. The lake biome case study — full build documentation
7. The estuary transition zone — the new frontier (post-Kickstarter content)

**Pre-sale mechanics:**
- Email list of 308 opt-ins + Patreon + YouTube community post
- Waitlist page on the website (build this in the web session)
- Early-bird pricing: $97 (first 50 buyers), $147 (next 50), $197 full price
- Delivery timeline: 90 days from pre-sale close

**Revenue estimate:** 308 email list + 25k subscribers + Patreon members. If 1% of email list converts at early-bird = $3,000+ before launch week ends.

## Tier 3 — Month 2–4

### Kickstarter — The Estuary Build
See full strategy in `kickstarter_estuary_campaign.md`.

**Summary:**
- Funding goal: $15,000
- Realistic range: $22,000–75,000+
- Timeline: 6 weeks warmup + 30 day campaign
- Zero physical fulfillment
- All reward delivery is digital or in-video

**The estuary build is the content plan regardless.** The Kickstarter funds it faster and builds community investment in the outcome. It is not a distraction from the build — it IS the build.

**Sequencing note:** Start Kickstarter warmup content in month 2. Campaign launches in month 3. This gives the Patreon push and first sponsorship deal time to establish before the campaign hits.

## Tier 4 — Post-Kickstarter (Month 4+)

### Merch — Print-on-Demand
**Platform:** Printful or Printify integrated with the website. Zero inventory. Zero upfront cost.

**Product direction:** Scientific, not fan merch.
- High-quality scientific illustration prints of organisms from the system
- Data visualization poster: miniBIOTA telemetry over time
- Organism field guide card sets (species from the biosphere, illustrated)
- "Founding Observer" hoodie or tee (limited, Kickstarter-era design)

**Why post-Kickstarter:**
- Campaign momentum drives the first wave of customers
- Backers are already emotionally invested — easiest first audience for merch
- Don't distract from Kickstarter preparation with a parallel build

**Revenue estimate:** $200–600/mo ongoing after initial launch wave.

### Course Delivery
After pre-sale validates demand, record and deliver. Content already exists across 338 videos — this is editing and structuring, not starting from zero.
