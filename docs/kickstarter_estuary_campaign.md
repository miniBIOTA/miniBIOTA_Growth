# Kickstarter — The Estuary Build

## The Anchor Concept

For the first time, miniBIOTA's freshwater and saltwater worlds will connect through a living salinity gradient. The estuary is not a product. It is an experiment — documenting the transition zone where life must adapt or die, in real time, inside a sealed system no one has built like this before.

This campaign is not asking people to buy something.
It is inviting them to help create a living world and leave a trace inside it.

**The campaign question:**
"How small can a world be and still remain alive?"

**The scientific hook:**
The estuary transition zone opens a new line of ecological knowledge with direct practical applications — including naturalistic amano shrimp breeding inside a fully documented, self-sustaining system. No one has done this at this level of documentation.

## Campaign Structure

### Funding Goal
**$15,000** — covers hardware, enclosure fabrication, sensor integration, and the first 90 days of the estuary build documented on camera.

Conservative target. Achievable within first 72 hours if warmup is done correctly. Every dollar above funds stretch goals that expand the world.

### Campaign Duration
30 days. No extensions.

### Launch Sequence
- **6 weeks before launch:** Warmup content begins. Estuary teaser video. Patreon members see design sketches first.
- **2 weeks before launch:** Kickstarter preview page live. Email list notified. Discord members get early access to the pledge page.
- **Launch day:** Video drops simultaneously on YouTube and Kickstarter.
- **Week 2:** Mid-campaign update video showing early build progress.
- **Final 48 hours:** Urgency push across all channels.

## Reward Tiers

### Tier 1 — Observer ($15)
*For people who want to watch history unfold.*
- Early access to all estuary videos before YouTube release
- Backer-only Field Journal posts: raw observations, failures, course corrections — written exactly like field notes
- Name listed permanently on the Founding Backers page on the website
- Zero physical fulfillment

### Tier 2 — Participant ($50)
*For people who want agency in what happens next.*
- Everything in Tier 1
- Vote on key decisions: which species enters the estuary transition zone first, which system upgrade happens next, which biome is studied at depth during the campaign
- Monthly backer-only livestream walkthrough of the full ecosystem
- Access to Director's Cut videos that go deeper than any public YouTube content
- Votes are from a scientifically vetted shortlist — backer agency without compromising the ecology

### Tier 3 — Legacy ($150)
*This is where the campaign magic lives.*
- Everything in Tier 2
- Name permanently embedded inside the estuary build: engraved on a biome access panel, embedded in a 3D-printed internal structural element, or inscribed on a visible "Biosphere Wall" inside the system
- Digital certificate showing exactly where their name exists inside miniBIOTA
- Name spoken aloud in the build video when that section is installed
- No shipping. No inventory. Humans crave permanence — this delivers it with zero logistics.

### Tier 4 — Steward ($300)
*Adoption without ownership — mirrors conservation sponsorship.*
- Everything in Tier 3
- "Biome Steward" or "Species Steward" status — associated with either a specific biome or organism group (plants, invertebrates, detritivores, etc.)
- Name appears on-screen whenever their biome or species group is featured in video
- Quarterly deep-dive update focused exclusively on their biome or species
- Fits the ecosystem philosophy: stewardship, not possession

### Tier 5 — Architect ($1,000 — limited to 10)
*Part of the design lineage.*
- Everything in Tier 4
- Name a non-species structure inside the estuary (a chamber, loop, channel, or system node)
- Credited as "Founding Architect" in every major video from the estuary build forward
- One private recorded Q&A walking them through the design philosophy and tradeoffs behind the estuary build
- These backers become evangelists. They will promote the campaign independently.

### Tier 6 — Worldbuilder ($5,000 — limited to 3)
*Patronage, not commerce.*
- Everything in Tier 5
- Executive Producer credit on the entire estuary build series
- Name or logo permanently etched into the main system frame
- Dedicated episode documenting the specific component or expansion their support enabled
- Annual private system walkthrough video recorded and sent just for them
- Target: museums, science educators, science-aligned companies, and conservation-minded donors

### Add-On — The Estuary Field Notes ($75)
Available to any backer at any tier.
- A comprehensive digital field guide documenting the entire estuary build process
- Covers: design decisions, failures, salinity calibration methodology, species selection rationale, transition zone ecology, and long-term outcomes
- Written in field-note style — honest, technical, and specific
- Delivered 6 months post-campaign when outcomes are documented
- This is the book equivalent — no printing, no shipping, maximum value

## Campaign Video Requirements

The video must be breathtaking. Backers cannot touch the ecosystem — they need to feel like they can see into another world.

**Structure:**
1. Open with the best existing footage from the system — 30 seconds, no narration, just sound
2. The question: "How small can a world be and still remain alive?"
3. What miniBIOTA is — 60 seconds, the existing system, what's been proven
4. The estuary: the specific build, the science, why it's never been done like this
5. The ask: what their support enables
6. Tiers explained in 90 seconds — visual, clean, no clutter
7. Close with the question again

**Key constraint:** No overproduction. The authenticity of the field-note aesthetic is the brand. It should feel like a scientist showing you something real, not a product launch.

## Pre-Campaign Warmup Content

6 weeks before launch, begin a warmup sequence across all channels. Do not announce the Kickstarter immediately.

- **Week 1:** "I've been designing something for a long time..." — a teaser post about the estuary concept, no details
- **Week 2:** Design sketch reveal on Patreon + Discord (members first)
- **Week 3:** Short video explaining the science of salinity transition zones and why they matter
- **Week 4:** "Here's what it would look like..." — first render or mock-up
- **Week 5:** Email list gets early access to the preview page
- **Week 6:** Launch

This sequence builds audience investment before they're ever asked to pledge.
